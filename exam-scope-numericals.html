1 · Cutting speed & feed (machining time)

Core: cutting speed V = πDN/1000 (D mm, N rpm, V m/min) → N = 1000V/(πD). Machining time T = L/(N·s), where L = length of tool travel = job length + approach D₁ + over-travel D₂ + a tool-geometry term, and s = feed per rev. These are the prof's "simple feed/cutting-speed" questions = Mehta Examples 1.1–1.6.

CS1 Turning — machining time MEHTA Ex 1.1

Turn a shaft Ø70 → Ø64 mm over length 200 mm at n=600 rpm, s=0.4 mm/rev, principal cutting-edge angle φ=45°. Find the machining time.

Show solution ▸
what goes wheredepth t=(70−64)/2 =3 mm · job 200 · tool-geometry term t·cotφ =3·cot45=3 · approach+overtravel D₁+D₂ =2+2 · n =600, s =0.4

L = job + t·cotφ + D₁ + D₂:

L = 200 + 3·cot45° + 2 + 2 = 200 + 3 + 4 = 207 mm

T = L/(n·s):

T = 207/(600·0.4) = 207/240 = 0.8625 min

T ≈ 0.86 min.

CS2 Pipe ring cut-off MEHTA Ex 1.2

Cut a ring from a pipe, OD D=100 mm, ID d=84 mm, at 250 rpm, feed 0.14 mm/rev. Machining time?

Show solution ▸
what goes whereparting depth = (D−d)/2 =(100−84)/2=8 · D₁+D₂ =2+2 · n =250, s =0.14

L = (D−d)/2 + D₁ + D₂ = 8 + 2 + 2 = 12 mm

T = L/(n·s) = 12/(250·0.14) = 12/35 = 0.342 min

T ≈ 0.34 min.

CS3 Drilling — machining time MEHTA Ex 1.3

Drill a Ø30 through-hole in a 30 mm plate at cutting speed 30 m/min, feed 0.1 mm/tooth. Machining time?

Show solution ▸
what goes wherepoint term (d/2)cotφ, φ=60° =(30/2)cot60=8.66 · D₁+D₂ =2+2 · drill has 2 teeth → srev=2×0.1 =0.2 mm/rev

L = 30 + 2 + 2 + (30/2)cot60° = 34 + 8.66 = 42.66 mm

N = 1000V/(πd) = 1000·30/(π·30) = 318.3 rpm · sm = N·srev = 318.3·0.2 = 63.66 mm/min

T = L/sm = 42.66/63.66 = 0.67 min

T ≈ 0.67 min. (Drill = 2 cutting teeth → feed/rev = 2 × feed/tooth.)

CS4 Plain milling — machining time MEHTA Ex 1.4

Mill a 200 mm job with a plain cutter D=40 mm, z=10 teeth, cutting speed 30 m/min, feed 0.08 mm/tooth, depth of cut t=4 mm. Machining time?

Show solution ▸
what goes whereapproach term √[t(D−t)] =√(4·36)=12 · D₁+D₂ =4 · feed/min sm=sz·z·N

L = 200 + √[t(D−t)] + D₁ + D₂ = 200 + 12 + 4 = 216 mm

N = 1000·30/(π·40) = 238.7 rpm · sm = 0.08·10·238.7 = 191.0 mm/min

T = 216/191 = 1.13 min

T ≈ 1.13 min.

CS5 Shaping — machining time MEHTA Ex 1.5

Shape a 100 mm wide × 200 mm long surface, feed/stroke 0.3 mm, cutting speed 20 m/min, return:cutting time ratio 1 : 1.25. Time to machine?

Show solution ▸
what goes wherestroke length L=1.2×job =1.2·200=240 mm · K=1.25 · width to cover B+B₁+B₂ =100+2+2 · feed/stroke =0.3

strokes/min n = 1000·V·K/[L(K+1)] = 1000·20·1.25/(240·2.25) = 46.3 → round to 50 strokes/min

T = (B+B₁+B₂)/(s·n) = (100+2+2)/(0.3·50) = 104/15 = 6.93 min

T ≈ 6.93 min. (Feed acts across the width; n from the quick-return ratio.)

CS6 Cylindrical grinding — time MEHTA Ex 1.6

External-traverse grind a Ø40, 210 mm long step; wheel Ø600, width 63 mm. Grinding time formula & method.

Show solution ▸
what goes whereT = L·h·K / (nwp·k·B·t) · L=workpiece length, h=allowance, B=wheel width, t=radial feed/stroke (0.01–0.025), k=0.7–0.85 (D≥20)

Grinding time: T = (L·h·K)/(nwp·k·B·t)

longitudinal feed sl = k·B per work-rev; radial feed t given at each stroke end; sum strokes over total allowance h.

Plug the wheel/work data into T = L·h·K/(nwp·k·B·t). (Grinding is the heaviest of the six; for the exam the turning/drilling/milling ones (CS1–CS4) are the likely ones.)

CS7 Cutting speed ↔ rpm (quickie) V=πDN/1000

Turn Ø100 mm at recommended V=40 m/min. Find N; if nearest available speed is 125 rpm, the actual V.

Show solution ▸

N = 1000V/(πD) = 1000·40/(π·100) = 127 rpm → pick 125 rpm

actual V = πDN/1000 = π·100·125/1000 = 39.3 m/min

N ≈ 127 → 125 rpm; V ≈ 39.3 m/min.

2 · Design of bases & tables

Hollow section I = [BH³−(B−2t)(H−2t)³]/12. Base/table on elastic foundation: k=125b, m=(k/4EI)^¼; slope θ summed from load + moment terms with chart factors K. Table "reduced stiffness" via the stiffener factor.

BT1 Base — maximum slope PPT M3 example

Base width b=60 cm, E=1.1×10⁶ kgf/cm², plate t=6 cm; distributed loads q₁=1.2, q₂=0.8 kgf/cm², moment M=5000 kgf·cm; geometry a₁=50, b₁=30, a₂=85, b₂=40 cm. Maximum slope?

Show solution ▸
what goes wherek=125b · I=bt³/12 · m=(k/4EI)^¼ · φ=a·m, B=b·m → chart KM,Kq1,Kq2

k = 125·60 = 7500 kgf/cm²; I = 60·6³/12 = 1080 cm⁴; m = (7500/(4·1.1×10⁶·1080))^¼ = 0.03544 cm⁻¹

φ₁=50m=1.77≈2, B₁=30m≈1.1; φ₂=85m=3.01≈3, B₂=40m≈1.4 → KM≈2.4, Kq1≈0.50, Kq2≈0.30

θq1=(1.2·60/7500)·0.50=0.00480; θq2=(0.8·60/7500)·0.30=0.00192; θM=(5000·0.03544³/7500)·2.4=7.1×10⁻⁵

θ = 0.00480+0.00192+0.00007 = 0.00679 rad ≈ 0.389°.

BT2 Hollow base — max slope MEHTA 3.8

Hollow rectangular steel base H=200 mm, width 1000 mm, wall 25 mm; loaded per Fig 3.36 (q₁=2.0, q₂=1.5 kgf/mm; applied moment 2×10⁵ kgf·mm; spans 400/250/500/1500 mm). E=2.1×10⁶. Maximum angle of slope?

Show solution ▸
what goes whereB=100, H=20, t=2.5 cm → I=[BH³−(B−2t)(H−2t)³]/12 · θ = ∫M dx/(EI), max at a support

I = [100·20³ − 95·15³]/12 = (800000−320625)/12 = 39 948 cm⁴; EI = 8.39×10¹⁰ kgf·cm²

θmax = AM/EI (moment-area from the Fig-3.36 reactions) ≈ ~10⁻⁴ rad

θmax ~ 10⁻⁴ rad — well within the ~0.5°/m machine-tool limit. (Same elastic-foundation method as BT1.)

BT3 Hollow table — reduced stiffness MEHTA 3.9

Hollow rectangular table 2 m × 60 cm × 9 cm; horizontal walls 15 mm joined by 10 mm vertical stiffeners at 400 mm. E=2.1×10⁶. Reduced (bending) stiffness?

Show solution ▸
what goes wherewall I=b·h³/12 (b=60, h=1.5) · stiffeners n=200/40=5 panels (4 internal) · factor s₁ (Eq 3.41)

Iwall = 60·1.5³/12 = 16.9 cm⁴; s₁ ≈ 31.9

EIbr = s·E·I = 31.9·2.1×10⁶·16.9 = ≈ 1.13×10⁹ kgf·cm²

EIbr ≈ 1.13×10⁹ kgf·cm².

3 · Design of slideways

Free-body of the carriage → normal reactions on the faces. Two-flat: B=Py, C=(Pzyp−Pyh)/b + G/2, A=Pz+G/2−(Pzyp−Pyh)/b. Width c′ = A/([pav]·L), [pav]=½[pmax]. V+flat: A,B on the V-faces (cosα/sinα), C on the flat. Lift-off when a reaction → 0.

SW1 Slideway width (two-flat) MEHTA 4.2

Turning Ø100; Pz=300, Py=130, Px=120 kgf; carriage G=150 kgf, length 30 cm, two flat CI ways. Find the face width. (Assume b, h, yp, f, [pav].)

Show solution ▸
what goes whereassume b=30, h=20, yp=15, f=0.1, [pav]=12.5 (=½·25 CI), L=30

m = (Pzyp−Pyh)/b = (300·15−130·20)/30 = 63.3

A = Pz+G/2−m = 300+75−63.3 = 311.7 kgf (largest)

c′ = A/([pav]·L) = 311.7/(12.5·30) = 0.83 cm · Q = Px+f(Pz+Py+G) = 120+58 = 178 kgf

width ≈ 0.83 cm (round to 1 cm); all reactions +ve → no lift-off.

SW2 Saddle lift-off (V+flat) MEHTA 4.1

Parting cut (Px=0), V half-angle α=50°, resultant force at 40° to the vertical, μ=0.5. At what d/b does the saddle lift off the V-face A?

Show solution ▸
what goes wherelift-off ⇒ A→0 ⇒ (d+b)/2b = (Py/Pz)·tanα = tanθ·tanα · θ=40° (tan=0.839), α=50° (tan=1.192) · μ doesn't enter

(d+b)/2b = 0.839·1.192 = 1.00 → d/b = 2(1.00)−1 = 1.0

d/b = 1.0 (saddle lifts when V-offset = way spacing). With the Pyh/b term → d/b ≈ 0.16.

SW3 Best slideway combination MEHTA 4.3

flat–flat vs flat–V vs V–V: which gives the least radial deflection of the cutting edge? Prove it.

Show solution ▸

Contact deflection δ = k·pav; tool-tip radial deflection δF = δB + (δA−δC)·h/b.

A V-way shares the radial load over two inclined faces (effective width d = m·cos²α + n·cos²β) → lower pav → lower δ; flat–flat reacts Py on one narrow face → largest δ.

V–V least, flat–V intermediate, flat–flat worst (δ ∝ pav; V splits load over two faces + self-centres).

SW4 Slideway reaction & pressure formulas PPT M3 (the prof's derivation)

Write the prof's closed-form reactions (V+flat) and the maximum-pressure expressions used to size a slideway.

Show solution ▸

V+flat reactions: A = [Pz(d+b)/2b + Pyh/b]cosα − Pysinα + (G/2)cosα · B = [Pz(d+b)/2b + Pyh/b]sinα + Pycosα + (G/2)sinα · C = Pz(b−d)/2b + G/2 − Pyh/b

Average pressure: pA = A/(a′L), etc. Max pressure (trapezoid, xA/L < 1/6): pmax = pav(1 + 6xA/L). Triangular (xA/L > 1/6): pmax = (4/3)·pav/(1 − 2xA/L).

Use these to find reactions → average pressure → xA → pmax, then check pmax ≤ [pmax]. Apply to numbers as in SW1/SW2.

4 · Calculation of deflection & associated

Box section I=(b⁴−(b−2t)⁴)/12; lateral cantilever δ=Ph³/(3EI). Angle of twist (open) θ=MtL/(G·It), It=β·⅓Σbt³. Machine-tool/spindle: compliance C=1/Ks+(1/KA)(1−x/L)²+(1/KB)(x/L)²; nose/run-out y=δB(1+c/l)+δA(c/l).

D1 Box column deflection MEHTA 3.6

CI box column 30×30 cm, wall 3 cm, height 200 cm, lateral load 200 kgf, E=1.2×10⁶. Max deflection?

Show solution ▸
what goes whereb=30, hole side=24 · P=200, h=200, E=1.2×10⁶ · lateral cantilever governs

I = (30⁴−24⁴)/12 = 39 852 cm⁴ · δ = Ph³/(3EI) = 200·200³/(3·1.2×10⁶·39 852) = ≈ 112 µm

δmax ≈ 112 µm (axial ≈ 1 µm, negligible).

D1b Column as cantilever (SI) PPT M3 example (s20)

A machine-tool column behaves like a cantilever beam. F=1500 N, L=0.8 m, E=210 GPa, I=6×10⁻⁶ m⁴. Find the deflection.

Show solution ▸
what goes whereF =1500 N · L =0.8 m · E =210×10⁹ Pa · I =6×10⁻⁶ m⁴ · cantilever δ=FL³/(3EI)

δ = FL³/(3EI) = 1500·0.8³/(3·210×10⁹·6×10⁻⁶) = (1500·0.512)/(3.78×10⁶) = 768/3.78×10⁶

δ = 0.000203 m = 0.203 mm

δ = 0.203 mm — too large for precision machining → the column must be stiffened/redesigned. (The prof's exact slide-20 example; same δ=FL³/3EI recipe as D1, in SI units.)

D2 Box structure + rib — wall thickness MEHTA 3.7

Mild-steel box, loaded wall 400×300 mm, P=200 kgf mid-wall, φ20 hole + rib/boss, permissible deflection 25 µm, E=2.1×10⁶. Find wall thickness.

Show solution ▸

plate δ ∝ Pa²/(Et³); t³ = α·P·a²/(E·δ) = 0.061·200·40²/(2.1×10⁶·0.0025) = 3.72 → t ≈ 1.5 cm

t ≈ 1.5 cm; boss height ≈ 3t + rib closes the hole-stiffness gap.

D3 Reduced bending rigidity (perp vs diagonal) MEHTA 3.5

Lathe bed 2 m × 1 m × 0.5 m; walls 50 mm, stiffeners 25 mm, CI E=1.2×10⁶. Compare perpendicular vs diagonal stiffeners.

Show solution ▸

Iwall = 100·5³/12 = 1042 cm⁴ · perpendicular EIbr ≈ 0.99×10¹⁰ · diagonal ≈ 3.0×10¹¹ kgf·cm²

Diagonal ≈ 30× stiffer for the same metal.

D4 I-section — angle of twist MEHTA 3.12 LAST PAPER Q6

I-section 140 tall, flange 80×20, web 10 (mm), Mt=1000 kgf·m, G=8×10⁵, L=100 cm. Find It, τmax, twist θ.

Show solution ▸
what goes whereβ=1.20 (I) · cm: flanges 8×2(×2), web 10×1 · tmax=2 (flange) · Mt=100000 kgf·cm

It = 1.20·⅓·[2·(8·2³)+(10·1³)] = 1.20·46 = 55.2 cm⁴

τmax = Mt·tmax/It = 100000·2/55.2 = 3623 kgf/cm² · θ = MtL/(G·It) = 0.2264 rad = 12.97°

It=55.2 cm⁴, τmax=3623 kgf/cm², θ=12.97°. (Angle of twist is the "deflection" here.)

D5 Closed box — limiting torque MEHTA 3.11

Closed box 100×60 mm, wall 10 mm, permissible shear 1000 kgf/cm². Limiting torque?

Show solution ▸

A₀ = (10−1)(6−1) = 45 cm² · Mlim = 2τA₀δ = 2·1000·45·1 = 90 000 kgf·cm = 900 kgf·m

Mlim = 900 kgf·m.

D6 Machine-tool compliance & displacement MEHTA 5.1

Between centres, L=750 mm, Py=150 kgf, tool 200 mm from tailstock; saddle/headstock/tailstock = 3000/4000/2500 kgf/mm. Compliance & cutting-point displacement?

Show solution ▸
what goes wherex = L−200 = 550 → x/L=0.733 · Ks=3000, KA=4000, KB=2500 · Py=150

C = 1/3000 + (1/4000)(0.267)² + (1/2500)(0.733)² = 0.566 µm/kgf · y = 0.566·150 = 85 µm

C ≈ 0.566 µm/kgf; displacement ≈ 85 µm.

D7 Deflection between centres MEHTA 5.2

Between centres L=500 mm, Ø80; Py=80 kgf, tool 200 mm from headstock; headstock 35 000, tailstock 15 000 kgf/cm. Cutting-point deflection?

Show solution ▸
what goes wherex/L=0.4 · K→kgf/mm (÷10): 3500, 1500 · only Py=80 bends the work

support y = (Py/KAKB)[KB(0.6)²+KA(0.4)²] ≈ 16.8 µm; +workpiece bending ≈ 4.5 → ≈ 21 µm

≈ 17 µm (supports), ~21 µm with workpiece bending.

D8 Spindle-nose deflection & optimum span MEHTA 5.3

Front-bearing yield δB=42.2 µm, rear δA=9.17 µm, overhang c=1, span l=3. Nose deflection & λopt?

Show solution ▸

y = δB(1+c/l)+δA(c/l) = 42.2·1.333 + 9.17·0.333 = ≈ 59 µm · λopt=l/c ≈ 3

y ≈ 59 µm; λopt ≈ 3 (front bearing dominates).

D9 Spindle run-out from clearance MEHTA 5.12

Bearings 20 cm apart; clearances 6 µm front, 4 µm rear; nose overhang 8 cm. Radial run-out?

Show solution ▸

max run-out = 10 µm.

D10 Lathe-bed deflection/stress check PPT M3 example

Workpiece Ø200×1500, tightening K=5000 N, Px/Py/Pz=2500/5000/10000 N, composite bed section (b₁=110, d₂=315 …), h=300 mm, allowable 12 N/mm². Check the bed.

Show solution ▸

centroid z̄=181.05, zmax=188.95 mm; Iyy=4.67×10⁸, Izz=4.19×10⁸, It=8.86×10⁸ mm⁴

σz=[(Pz−G)L/4+(Px+K)c]zmax/Iyy=2.58 · σy=(PyL/4)ymax/Izz=0.21 · τ=MT·max(y,z)/It=0.53 N/mm²

σp,max = (σz+σy)/2 + ½√[(σz−σy)²+4τ²] = 2.79 N/mm²

σp,max = 2.79 < 12 N/mm² → bed SAFE. (The prof's signature structures problem.)
