CLO1

Drives & Kinematics

Q1.[4]

Sketch a block diagram of a lathe's kinematic chain and label the speed chain and the feed chain.

Model answer — what to draw
Draw: a flow chain. Speed chain: Motor → belt/pulley → speed gearbox (headstock) → Spindle → workpiece. Branch from the spindle for the feed chain: spindle → tumbler/change gears → feed gearbox → lead screw (threading) / feed rod → apron → carriage & cross-slide. Label each box; mark the half-nut on the lead screw. State that the feed is geared to spindle rotation (mm/rev).
Q2.[4]

With sketches, show the generatrix and directrix used to produce (a) a taper (cone) by turning and (b) a screw thread.

Model answer — what to draw
Draw: (a) Cone: generatrix = straight tool path inclined to the axis (top-slide swivelled); directrix = the circle from work rotation. Label half-cone angle. (b) Thread: generatrix = the tool's thread-form profile; directrix = a helix (work rotation coupled to tool axial feed via the lead screw). Label lead/pitch and the helix.
CLO2

Speed & Feed Diagrams

Q3.[5]

Draw the structural (ray) diagram for a 6-speed (3×2) gearbox and explain what the rays represent.

Model answer — what to draw
Draw: three vertical shaft lines (I, II, III). Shaft I→II shows a 3-ray group (three transmission ratios), shaft II→III a 2-ray group → 3×2 = 6 output speeds. Each ray slope = a transmission ratio (up = step-up, down = step-down); equal log spacings = GP step φ. Mark that ratios stay within ¼ ≤ i ≤ 2. The diagram fixes the number of speeds and how ratios are distributed across stages.
Q4.[4]

Sketch a speed diagram (logarithmic rpm scale) for a 6-speed GP drive and show why GP gives equal spacing.

Model answer — what to draw
Draw: a vertical log-rpm scale with the six speeds n₁…n₆ marked. Because nₖ₊₁ = φ·nₖ, log nₖ₊₁ − log nₖ = log φ = constant → the points are equally spaced on the log scale. Show the constant gap = log φ. Contrast (note) that an AP would give unequal gaps.
CLO3

Guideways

Q5.[4]

Sketch and label the cross-sections of flat, vee and dovetail guideways.

Model answer — what to draw
Draw: Flat — rectangular bearing face + a gib/retaining strip (label gib). Vee — symmetric V (label included angle, usually 90°) with the slide sitting in it. Dovetail — trapezoidal groove with inclined retaining faces + adjusting gib (label the angle ≈55°). Note one merit under each: flat = load area, V = self-aligning, dovetail = resists lift-off.
Q6.[4]

Sketch the free-body of a carriage on a combination of two flat slideways, showing all forces and reactions.

Model answer — what to draw
Draw: carriage on two flat ways spacing b. Cutting forces at the tool: P_z (down), P_y (radial), P_x (axial); weight G at centre. Reactions: A and C vertical (the two ways), B = P_y on the side retaining face, friction f·A, f·B, f·C; pulling force Q along the feed. Mark cutting-point height h and offset y_p. (Equilibrium: ΣX, ΣY, ΣZ, ΣM_x.)
CLO4

Structures & Spindles

Q7.[3]

Sketch a closed-box section and an I-section and mark which is preferred for torsion and which for bending.

Model answer — what to draw
Draw: a hollow rectangular box (mark enclosed area A₀, wall δ) — best in torsion (J ∝ A₀²). An I-section (mark flanges far from the neutral axis) — best in bending (max I per weight). One line each on why.
Q8.[4]

Sketch the design diagram of a spindle carried on two bearings and label l, c, a, b, P₁, P₂ and the reactive moment M_r.

Model answer — what to draw
Draw: a shaft on a rear support (hinge) and a front support; supported span l, overhang c to the nose. Gear force P₂ inside the span located by a and b (a+b=l); nose force P₁ at the tip. If the front is a sleeve bearing, replace it by a hinge + reactive moment M_r. Mark reactions R_A, R_B.
CLO5

Vibration

Q9.[4]

Sketch the regenerative-chatter wave model and label the inner and outer modulation and the varying chip thickness.

Model answer — what to draw
Draw: two overlapping wavy lines — the outer wave y(t−τ) left by the previous pass and the inner wave y(t) being cut now — with a phase shift between them. The instantaneous chip thickness = y(t) − m·y(t−τ) (m = overlap factor). Show that the phase lag makes chip thickness and force fluctuate, feeding energy in. Label τ = time per rev/tooth.
Q10.[4]

Sketch a stability-lobe diagram and label the stable/unstable zones and blim,min.

Model answer — what to draw
Draw: axes — limiting depth of cut blim (y) vs spindle speed n (x). Show the scalloped lobes; below the lowest scallop a horizontal line = blim,min (stable at any speed). Mark stable regions (under the curve) and unstable (above). Note: pick a speed under a lobe peak to allow a bigger stable depth.
Q11.[3]

Sketch a dynamic vibration absorber (tuned-mass damper) fitted to a tool/spindle and state its tuning.

Model answer — what to draw
Draw: the main mass (tool/spindle, stiffness k, mass M) with a small auxiliary mass m on a spring k₂ (and a damper c) attached. Tune so the absorber's natural frequency ≈ the troublesome forcing/chatter frequency; it then oscillates out of phase and absorbs the energy, cutting the main amplitude. Label M, m, k₂, c.
