Numericals = 40% of the paper, drawn from ch 3 (structures), 4 (guideways), 5 (spindles). This maps every numerical-bearing topic: where it sits in the lecture PPT, where it lives in Mehta, the type of numerical, and whether it appeared on the last paper.

Three things that decide where you spend time

1. Power screws are NOT in the PPT. Module 3's slides run structures → guideways and stop at slideway contact-compliance (slide 153). Lead-screw (Mehta 4.9) and ball-screw (4.10) numericals — and the hydrostatic-pad (4.6/4.7) and slider-bearing (4.4) problems — were not lectured. They're textbook-only → low exam priority. The guideway numericals the prof actually taught are slideway forces, pressure distribution, and slideway stiffness.

2. Speed-gearbox (ch 2) is numerical too. Even though "numericals = ch 3/4/5", Module 2 has solved GP / ray-diagram problems and the last paper's Q5 was exactly that. Don't drop it.

3. The prof's own flags (viva/intel): design of bases & tables, design of slideways, calculation of deflection — all in ch 3/4, all in the tables below.

Bottom line — what to drill vs skip

Drill hard (taught + prof-flagged + on the paper)

Section torsion (ch3, was Q6) · bases & tables deflection/slope (ch3) · slideway forces & face width (ch4) · machine-tool compliance / spindle deflection (ch5) · plus the GP speed gearbox (ch2, was Q5). Reduced bending rigidity and lathe-bed stresses (ch3) are taught and fair game.

Lower priority (in Mehta, not in the PPT)

Power screws — lead screw (4.9) and ball screw (4.10); hydrostatic pad (4.6/4.7) and slider bearing (4.4); pulling-force conv-vs-ball (4.8); hydrostatic/aerodynamic spindle bearings (5.9/5.10). Know the method (they're in the drills/guide) but don't sink your hours here — the lecturer didn't cover them.
