A · Machine-tool compliance (prof-flagged: deflection)

C = 1/Ks + (1/KA)(1−x/L)² + (1/KB)(x/L)², x from the headstock A; y = C·Py. K in kgf/mm → C in mm/kgf (×1000 = µm/kgf). Only radial Py bends the work.

A1 Compliance & displacement MEHTA 5.1

Between centres, L=750 mm; radial Py=150 kgf, tool 200 mm from tailstock. Stiffness saddle/headstock/tailstock = 3000/4000/2500 kgf/mm. Find compliance & cutting-point displacement.

Show solution ▸
what goes wherex = L−200 = 550 mm → x/L =0.733 · Ks =3000, KA =4000, KB =2500 · Py =150

C = 1/3000 + (1/4000)(0.267)² + (1/2500)(0.733)² = 0.566 µm/kgf

y = 0.566·150 = 85 µm

C ≈ 0.566 µm/kgf; displacement ≈ 85 µm.

+ workpiece bending ywp=Pya²b²/(3EIL) ≈ 4.5 µm → total ≈ 21.3 µm

≈ 16.8 µm (supports); ~21 µm incl. workpiece bending. Convert kgf/cm → kgf/mm (÷10) first.

B · Spindle-nose deflection & optimum span

Nose y = δB(1+c/l) + δA(c/l); front bearing B dominates (1+c/l amplification). δ=R/K from beam statics. Optimum span λ=l/c ≈ 3–5 (≥2.5 for anti-friction).

B1 Nose deflection & optimum span MEHTA 5.3

Spindle: front-bearing yield δB=42.2 µm, rear δA=9.17 µm; overhang c=1, span l=3. Find the nose deflection and state λopt.

Show solution ▸
what goes whereδB =42.2 (front, ×(1+c/l)) · δA =9.17 (rear, ×c/l) · c/l =0.333 → 1+c/l =1.333

y = 42.2·1.333 + 9.17·0.333 = 56.3 + 3.1 = ≈ 59 µm

y ≈ 59 µm; λopt=l/c ≈ 3 (bending ↑, support-compliance ↓ → min sum).

C · Run-out from bearing clearance

Same form: run-out = δB(1+c/l) + δA(c/l), but δ's are radial clearances; take them opposite for the worst case. Front clearance dominates.

C1 Spindle-nose run-out MEHTA 5.12

Two bearings 20 cm apart; radial clearances 4 µm (rear) & 6 µm (front); nose overhang 8 cm from the 6-µm bearing. Find the radial run-out.

Show solution ▸
what goes whereδB =6 (front), δA =4 (rear) · c/l = 8/20 =0.4 → 1+c/l =1.4

run-out = 6·1.4 + 4·0.4 = 8.4 + 1.6 = 10 µm

max run-out = 10 µm.

Bearings 25 cm apart; clearances 8 µm (front), 5 µm (rear); overhang 10 cm.

Show solution ▸
what goes whereδB =8, δA =5, c/l = 10/25 =0.4

run-out = 8·1.4 + 5·0.4 = 11.2 + 2.0 = 13.2 µm

max run-out = 13.2 µm.

D · Hollow spindle — combined bending + torsion

Torque Mt = 974·P/n (P kW, n rpm → kgf·m). Front bearing carries the biggest bending moment (P₁·c). Combine load planes vectorially, size do, di from Eq 5.38 (α=di/do).

D1 Hollow-spindle diameters MEHTA 5.4

Hollow lathe spindle do/di=2. Power 4.5 kW at 700 rpm. P₂=215, T₂=78 kgf at gear; P₁=210, T₁=60 kgf at nose; span 170, overhang 80 mm. Find do, di.

Show solution ▸
what goes whereMt = 974·P/n · α=di/do =0.5 · front-bearing moment from P₁·c, T₁·c · σ₋₁ ≈2500, n ≈1.5 (table)

Mt = 974·4.5/700 = 6.26 kgf·m = 626 kgf·cm

Mb at front = √((P₁c)²+(T₁c)²) = √(16800²+4800²) ≈ 1747 kgf·cm; Eq 5.38 → do³≈30 cm³

strength: do≈31, di≈16 mm; stiffness-governed practical pick (Table 5.3) do=70, di=35 mm.

E · Sleeve / journal bearing — p, v, pv

Projected pressure p = P/(l·d) (NOT πdl). Surface speed v = πdn/60 (d in m). pv → material table (high v, low p → tin bronze / white metal).

E1 Sleeve-bearing material MEHTA 5.5

Precision lathe spindle journal d=80 mm, n up to 2500 rpm, max bearing load 30 kgf, l/d=1. Select a sleeve material.

Show solution ▸
what goes whered =8 cm (pressure), =0.08 m (speed); l=d=8 cm · P =30 · n =2500

p = 30/(8·8) = 0.469 kgf/cm² · v = π·0.08·2500/60 = 10.47 m/s · pv = 4.9

high v, low p → tin bronze (within [p],[pv]).

Show solution ▸

p=0.50, v=10.47, pv=5.24 → tin bronze / white metal.

F · Hydrodynamic journal bearing

ψ→c=ψd/2 → Sommerfeld S₀ → viscosity μ (Eq 5.47) → ε → hmin=c(1−ε). Chart-read values flagged (table).

F1 Hydrodynamic bearing design MEHTA 5.6

Cu–Pb journal bearing d=60 mm, P=150 kgf, n=3000 rpm, hcr=10 µm, need hmin/hcr>1.1. Find the design parameters.

Show solution ▸
what goes wherel/d=1 (l=6 cm) · ψ =0.001 (table) → c=ψd/2=30 µm · S₀ =1.0 (table)

p = 150/36 = 4.17 kgf/cm²; ω=314 rad/s; μ ≥ pψ²/(ωS₀) = 1.33×10⁻⁴ kgf·s/m² ≈ 1.3 cP

ε ≈ 0.53 (table) → hmin = c(1−ε) = 30(0.47) = 14.2 µm; ratio 1.42 > 1.1 ✓

l/d=1, ψ=0.001 (c=30 µm), oil μ≈1.3 cP, ε≈0.53, hmin≈14 µm. Acceptable.

F2 Babbit bearing — capacity & heat MEHTA 5.7

Babbit journal d=80 mm, n=2000 rpm, p≤8 kgf/cm². Load capacity? Check heating (max 70 °C, ambient 40 °C).

Show solution ▸

tb = 40 + W/(k·A) ≈ 40 + 43/(34.9·0.128) = ≈ 50 °C < 70 ✓

load ≈ 512 kgf; tb ≈ 50 °C → no forced cooling.

G · Hydrostatic / aerodynamic / multiple-wedge (book-only — lower priority)

Not in the lecture PPT — included for completeness. Hydrostatic: pp=2p₀ optimum, A=W/(CLp₀). Aerodynamic: P=0.5·pa·l·d·K₁·K₂. Multiple-wedge: unloaded P₀ + Eq 5.63.

G1 Cylindrical hydrostatic pad MEHTA 5.9 · book only

Cylindrical-pad hydrostatic bearing r/R=0.7. W=200 kgf, n=1000 rpm, pp=20 kgf/cm², gap h=0.005 cm, μ=4×10⁻⁷. Required restrictor opening?

Show solution ▸
what goes wherepp=2p₀ → p₀ =10 · CL ≈0.5 (table, r/R=0.7) · A=W/(CLp₀)

A = 200/(0.5·10) = 40 cm²; pad flow Q = p₀h³(1/CF′)/μ ≈ 6.25 cm³/s

orifice: Q = Cda₀√(2Δp/ρ), Δp=10 → a₀ → d₀ ≈ 0.53 mm

p₀=10 kgf/cm², A=40 cm², restrictor ≈ 0.53 mm dia.

d ≈ 22 mm, l ≈ 17.7 mm.
