P1 Lathe-bed strength check FROM PPT · M3 s.59–65

Workpiece d=200 mm, L=1500 mm, tightening load K=5000 N. Cutting force Px=2500, Py=5000, Pz=10000 N. Composite bed section: b₁=110, b₂=20, b₃=115, d₁=30, d₂=315, d₃=25 mm (+ A=365, B=145, C=275, D=235, E=370, F=140). Height of centres h=300 mm. Allowable bed stress 12 N/mm². Check the bed strength.

Show solution ▸
what goes wheresection = bottom flange 30×110 + web 315×20 + top flange 25×115 · vertical bending uses Iyy, horizontal uses Izz, torsion uses It=Iyy+Izz · c = h + hfp

Centroid (z̄ = ΣAizi/ΣAi):

z̄ = 181.05 mm → z₁=181.05, z₂=188.95, zmax=188.95 mm; ymax=185.0 mm

Section properties:

Iyy=4.665×10⁸, Izz=4.190×10⁸, It=Iyy+Izz=8.855×10⁸ mm⁴

Vertical bending: σz,max = [(Pz−G)L/4 + (Px+K)c]·zmax/Iyy = 2.58 N/mm²

Horizontal bending: σy,max = (PyL/4)·ymax/Izz = 0.21 N/mm²

Torsion: MT=(Pzd/2 + Pyh); τmax=MT·max(ymax,zmax)/It = 0.53 N/mm²

Principal stress: σp,max = (σz+σy)/2 + ½√[(σz−σy)²+4τ²]

σp,max = 2.79 N/mm²

σp,max = 2.79 N/mm² < 12 N/mm² → bed is SAFE in strength.

P2 Base / table — maximum slope FROM PPT · M3 s.83–87

Base width b=60 cm, plate material E=1.1×10⁶ kgf/cm², plate thickness t=6 cm. Distributed loads q₁ᵐ=1.2, q₂ᵐ=0.8 kgf/cm². Applied bending moment M=5000 kgf·cm. Geometry a₁=50, b₁=30, a₂=85, b₂=40 cm. Find the maximum slope. (Plate on elastic foundation; read KM, Kq from the charts.)

Show solution ▸
what goes wherefoundation k=125b · I=bt³/12 · m=(k/4EI)^¼ · φ=a·m, B=b·m feed the chart → KM, Kq1, Kq2

Step 1 — k & I: k = 125b = 125·60 = 7500 kgf/cm²; I = bt³/12 = 60·6³/12 = 1080 cm⁴

Step 2 — m: m = (k/4EI)^¼ = (7500/(4·1.1×10⁶·1080))^¼ = 0.03544 cm⁻¹

Step 3 — φ, B: φ₁=a₁m=1.77≈2, B₁=b₁m=1.06≈1.1; φ₂=a₂m=3.01≈3, B₂=b₂m=1.42≈1.4

Step 4 — chart factors: KM≈2.4, Kq1≈0.50, Kq2≈0.30

Step 5 — slope components:

θq1=(q₁ᵐb/k)Kq1=(1.2·60/7500)·0.50=0.00480 rad · θq2=(0.8·60/7500)·0.30=0.00192 rad · θM=(Mm³/k)KM=7.12×10⁻⁵ rad

Step 6 — total: θ = 0.00480 + 0.00192 + 0.0000712 = 0.00679 rad

θ ≈ 0.00679 rad ≈ 0.389°.

A · Section torsion — open & closed

Open: It = β·⅓·Σb·t³ (β: rect 1.0, channel 1.12, I 1.20), τmax = Mt·tmax/It (tmax=thickest), θ = MtL/(G·It). Closed: τ = Mt/(2A₀δ), Mlim = 2τA₀δ, A₀ = mid-line area.

A1 I-section torsion MEHTA 3.12 LAST PAPER Q6

I-section 140 tall, flange 80×20, web 10 (mm). Mt=1000 kgf·m, G=8×10⁵ kgf/cm², L=100 cm. Find It, τmax, θ.

Show solution ▸
what goes whereβ =1.20 (I) · rects (cm): flanges 8×2(×2), web 10×1 (web=140−2·20=100mm) · tmax =2 (flange) · Mt =100000 kgf·cm

It = 1.20·⅓·[2·(8·2³)+(10·1³)] = 1.20·⅓·138 = 55.2 cm⁴

τmax = 100000·2/55.2 = 3623 kgf/cm² · θ = 100000·100/(8×10⁵·55.2) = 0.2264 rad = 12.97°

It=55.2 cm⁴, τmax=3623 kgf/cm², θ=12.97°.

Mlim = 2τA₀δ = 2·1000·45·1 = 90 000 kgf·cm = 900 kgf·m

A6 Compound section — stiffness gain MEHTA 3.4 (=Ex 3.1)

A channel (wall δ) has a box/angle welded on (added member wall 2δ). Find the factor by which torsional stiffness improves and shear stress changes vs the channel alone.

Show solution ▸
what goes wherechannel It = 1.12·⅓·δ³(2b+a) · compound adds member at 2δ → (2δ)³=8δ³

stiffness factor = It,comp/It,chan = (3b+8a)/(2b+a)

shear-stress factor = 2(2b+a)/(3b+8a) <1 (δmax jumps from δ to 2δ)

Stiffness ↑ by (3b+8a)/(2b+a); stress changes by 2(2b+a)/(3b+8a). Plug the figure's a,b for numbers. (This is the slide-36 / Example 3.1 problem.)

B · Reduced bending rigidity (stiffeners)

Single wall Iwall = b·h³/12 (b=wall height, h=thickness). EIbr = s·E·Iwall, s from Eq 3.41 (perpendicular) / 3.42 (diagonal). Headline: diagonal ≈ 30× stiffer than perpendicular.

B1 Lathe bed — perp vs diagonal stiffeners MEHTA 3.5

Lathe bed 2 m long, 1 m high, 0.5 m wide; walls 50 mm, stiffeners 25 mm. CI, E=1.2×10⁶. Compare reduced bending rigidity for perpendicular vs diagonal stiffeners.

Show solution ▸
what goes wherewall: b =100 cm (height), h =5 cm (thickness) · E =1.2×10⁶ · s from Eq 3.41/3.42 (state assumed)

Iwall = 100·5³/12 = 1042 cm⁴

perpendicular: EIbr ≈ 0.99×10¹⁰ · diagonal: EIbr ≈ 3.0×10¹¹ kgf·cm²

Diagonal ≈ 30× stiffer. The headline answer to quote.

B2 Hollow table — reduced stiffness MEHTA 3.9

Hollow rectangular table 2 m × 60 cm × 9 cm; horizontal walls 15 mm joined by 10 mm vertical stiffeners at 400 mm. E=2.1×10⁶. Find the reduced (bending) stiffness.

Show solution ▸
what goes wherewall b =60, h =1.5 cm · stiffeners n = 200/40 = 5 panels (4 internal) · use perpendicular Eq 3.41

C · Box column / structure deflection

Hollow box I = (b⁴−(b−2t)⁴)/12 (square) or [BH³−(B−2t)(H−2t)³]/12. Lateral cantilever δ = P·h³/(3EI) (governs); axial δ = Ph/(EA) (tiny).

C1 Box column deflection MEHTA 3.6

CI box column 30×30 cm outer, wall 3 cm, height 200 cm, lateral load 200 kgf, E=1.2×10⁶. Max deflection?

Show solution ▸
what goes whereb =30, hole side =24 (30−2·3) · P =200, h =200 cm, E =1.2×10⁶

I = (30⁴−24⁴)/12 = 39 852 cm⁴

δ = 200·200³/(3·1.2×10⁶·39 852) = 0.0112 cm ≈ 112 µm

δmax ≈ 112 µm (lateral governs); axial ≈ 1 µm.

C2 Box-structure with rib MEHTA 3.7

Mild-steel box, loaded wall 400×300 mm, P=200 kgf at mid-wall; φ20 hole strengthened by rib + boss; permissible deflection 25 µm, E=2.1×10⁶. Find wall thickness.

Show solution ▸
what goes whereplate δ ∝ Pa²/(Et³) · P =200, a =40, b =30 cm (a/b=1.33→α≈0.061), [δ] =0.0025 cm

t³ = α·P·a²/(E·δ) = 0.061·200·40²/(2.1×10⁶·0.0025) = 3.72 → t ≈ 1.55 cm

δ ≈ 1.5 cm (≈15 mm); boss height ≈ 3δ + rib closes the hole-stiffness gap.

D · Bases & tables — slope (prof-flagged)

E · Stiffness k=F/δ & optimum l²/h

k = F/δ (watch units). Optimum (l²/h) = 6E[δ]/[σ]: below it → strength governs, above it → stiffness governs (the usual machine-tool case).

F · Model scaling & frequency-to-avoid

Model: fs/fm = (1/λ)·√(Esρm/Emρs) (1/λ outside the root). Frequency to avoid: f = nZ/60, n = 1000V/(πD); evaluate at both extremes.

F1 Perspex model frequency MEHTA 3.10

Perspex model at 1:10 (λ=10) has bending natural frequency 150 Hz. Find the steel structure's frequency. (EPerspex≈0.03×10⁶, ρ_Perspex=1.2, ρ_steel=7.8.)

Show solution ▸
what goes whereλ =10 (outside root) · Es/Em =70 · ρm/ρs =0.154

fs = 150·(1/10)·√(70·0.154) = 150·0.1·3.28 = ≈ 49 Hz

fsteel ≈ 49 Hz.

F2 Frequency range to avoid MEHTA 3.2

Vertical milling machine. Carbide cutters D=50–250 mm, Z=8–14; HSS D=80–250 mm, Z=10–26. Cutting speed 30 m/min (HSS-MS) to 500 m/min (carbide-Al). Find the tooth-excitation frequency range to avoid.

Show solution ▸
what goes wheref = nZ/60, n = 1000V/(πD) · lowest: D=250,V=30,Z=8 · highest: D=80,V=500,Z=26

fmin: n=1000·30/(π·250)=38.2 → f=38.2·8/60 = 5.1 Hz

fmax: n=1000·500/(π·80)=1989 → f=1989·26/60 = 862 Hz

Avoid ≈ 5 Hz → 862 Hz — whole band; fit a tunable damper since design alone can't clear it.
