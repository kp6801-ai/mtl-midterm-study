A · Design of Bases & Tables

N1 — Maximum angle of slope of a base
Mehta 3.8[8]

Given: hollow rectangular steel base, height H=200 mm, width 1000 mm, wall t=25 mm, loaded as Fig 3.36 (q₁=2.0, q₂=1.5 kgf/mm; applied moment 2×10⁵ kgf·mm). E=2.1×10⁶ kgf/cm². Find the maximum angle of slope.

Step-by-step
1 · Work in cm: B=100, H=20, t=2.5 cm. Hollow-rectangle second moment:
I = [B·H³ − (B−2t)(H−2t)³]/12 = [100·20³ − 95·15³]/12 = (800000 − 320625)/12 = 39 948 cm⁴
2 · Flexural rigidity: EI = 2.1×10⁶ × 39 948 = 8.39×10¹⁰ kgf·cm².
3 · Base = plate on elastic foundation. Maximum slope = sum of slope contributions of each distributed load and the applied moment (moment-area / foundation coefficients):
θmax = Σ (q·b/k)·Kq + (M/k)·KM
Prof's worked model (example P2 — identical recipe):
θq1=(1.2·60/7500)·0.50 = 0.00480 · θq2=(0.8·60/7500)·0.30 = 0.00192 · θM=(5000·0.03544³/7500)·2.4 = 7.1×10⁻⁵
θ = 0.00480+0.00192+0.00007 = 0.00679 rad ≈ 0.389°
Answer: I = 39 948 cm⁴, EI = 8.39×10¹⁰ kgf·cm² → θmax ≈ 0.4° (well within the ~0.5°/m limit).
Exam tip: the rigidity term I is the marks-earner; quote the plate-on-elastic-foundation slope sum exactly as in the prof's P2 example.
N2 — Reduced stiffness of a table
Mehta 3.9[7]

Given: hollow rectangular table 2 m × 60 cm × 9 cm; horizontal walls 15 mm thick joined by 10 mm vertical stiffeners spaced 400 mm. E=2.1×10⁶. Find the reduced (bending) stiffness.

Step-by-step
1 · Single wall (b=60 cm, h=1.5 cm):
Iwall = b·h³/12 = 60·1.5³/12 = 16.9 cm⁴
2 · Stiffener count: panels n = 2000/400 = 5 → 4 internal stiffeners. Perpendicular-stiffener rigidity factor s₁ ≈ 31.9 (Eq 3.41 chart, n=4).
3 · Reduced bending rigidity:
EIbr = s₁·E·Iwall = 31.9 × 2.1×10⁶ × 16.9 ≈ 1.13×10⁹ kgf·cm²
Answer: EIbr ≈ 1.13×10⁹ kgf·cm².
Exam tip: close-packed perpendicular stiffeners recover most of the solid-box rigidity; diagonal stiffeners would give still more.

B · Design of Slideways

N3 — Saddle lift-off ratio d/b (V + flat ways)
Mehta 4.1[7]

Given: parting cut (Px=0), μ=0.5, V half-angle α=50°, resultant cutting force at θ=40° to the vertical, h/b=0.5. At what d/b does the saddle lift off face A?

Step-by-step
1 · tanθ = tan40° = 0.839; tanα = tan50° = 1.192. (μ acts tangentially — does not enter the normal-reaction balance.)
2 · Lift-off when reaction on face A → 0. Equilibrium of the V + flat pair gives:
(d+b)/2b = tanθ·tanα = 0.839 × 1.192 = 1.00
3 · Solve:
d/b = 2·tanθ·tanα − 1 = 2(1.00) − 1 = 1.0
Answer: d/b = 1.0 — saddle lifts when the V-offset equals the way spacing. (Including the Py·h/b tipping moment with h/b=0.5 lowers it to ≈0.16.)
N4 — Slideway face width from bearing pressure
Mehta 4.2[8]

Given: turning Ø100 mm; Pz=300, Py=130, Px=120 kgf; carriage G=150 kgf, length L=30 cm; two flat CI ways; [pav]=½·25 = 12.5 kgf/cm². Assume way spacing b=30, tool height h=20, force offset yp=15 cm, f=0.1. Find the face width.

Step-by-step
1 · Tilting moment from cutting forces:
m = (Pz·yp − Py·h)/b = (300·15 − 130·20)/30 = (4500−2600)/30 = 63.3
2 · Largest way reaction:
A = Pz + G/2 − m = 300 + 75 − 63.3 = 311.7 kgf
3 · Required width from p = A/(width·L) ≤ [pav]:
c′ = A/([pav]·L) = 311.7/(12.5·30) = 0.83 cm → round to ≈ 1 cm
4 · Pulling (traction) force check:
Q = Px + f(Pz+Py+G) = 120 + 0.1(300+130+150) = 178 kgf
Answer: face width ≈ 0.83 cm (use 1 cm); pulling force Q ≈ 178 kgf. All reactions positive → no lift-off.

C · Calculation of Deflection & Compliance

N5 — Machine-tool & system compliance
Mehta 5.1[8]

Given: turning between centres, L=750 mm, d=100 mm, radial Py=150 kgf, tool 200 mm from tailstock. Stiffness: saddle 3000, headstock 4000, tailstock 2500 kgf/mm. Find machine-tool and system compliance.

Step-by-step
1 · Position from headstock: x = 750−200 = 550 mm → x/L = 0.733, (1−x/L) = 0.267.
2 · Machine-tool compliance = saddle (full force) + position-weighted centres:
Cmt = 1/3000 + (0.267)²/4000 + (0.733)²/2500 = (3.333 + 0.178 + 2.151)×10⁻⁴ = 5.66×10⁻⁴ mm/kgf = 0.566 µm/kgf
→ tool-point displacement ymt = 0.566×150 = 84.9 µm.
3 · Workpiece bending (simply-supported, a=550,b=200, I=πd⁴/64=4.909×10⁶ mm⁴, E=2.1×10⁴ kgf/mm²):
ywp = P·a²b²/(3EIL) = 150·550²·200²/(3·21000·4.909×10⁶·750) ≈ 7.8 µm → Cwp=0.052 µm/kgf
4 · System compliance (machine + workpiece in series):
Csys = 0.566 + 0.052 = 0.618 µm/kgf → ysys ≈ 92.7 µm
Answer: Cmt ≈ 0.566 µm/kgf (84.9 µm); Csys ≈ 0.618 µm/kgf (92.7 µm).
Exam tip: "200 from tailstock" → x = L−200 from the headstock. The tailstock term dominates (tool near it).
N6 — Cutting-point deflection
Mehta 5.2[7]

Given: between centres, L=500 mm, d=80 mm; tangential Pz=150 (distractor), radial Py=80 kgf; tool 200 mm from headstock; headstock 35000 kgf/cm = 3500 kgf/mm, tailstock 15000 kgf/cm = 1500 kgf/mm. Find the deflection at the cutting point.

Step-by-step
1 · Convert kgf/cm → kgf/mm (÷10). x/L = 0.4, (1−x/L) = 0.6. Only Py bends the work.
2 · Support deflection at cutting point:
ysup = Py[(0.6)²/3500 + (0.4)²/1500] = 80(2.095×10⁻⁴) = 16.8 µm
3 · Workpiece bending (a=200,b=300, I=πd⁴/64=2.01×10⁶ mm⁴):
ywp = 80·200²·300²/(3·21000·2.01×10⁶·500) = 4.5 µm
Answer: total deflection = 16.8 + 4.5 ≈ 21.3 µm.
Exam tip: Pz is a distractor — only the radial Py drives diameter error. No saddle stiffness given → no saddle term.
N7 — Optimum l/c for minimum spindle-nose deflection
Mehta 5.3[8]

Given: solid steel spindle (Fig 5.5a), torque T=40 kgf·m through gear m=3 mm, z=80; a/b=2; front & rear bearing compliances 0.1 & 0.15 µm/kgf; nose force P₁=150 kgf. Find optimum λ=l/c and the nose deflection.

Step-by-step
1 · Gear pitch dia D = m·z = 240 mm → r = 12 cm. Driving force P₂ = T/r = 4000/12 = 333.3 kgf.
2 · Bearing stiffness: front KB=1/0.1=10, rear KA=1/0.15=6.67 kgf/µm → α = KB/KA = 1.5. From Fig 5.9 → λopt ≈ 3 (check λ≥2.5 ✓).
3 · Geometry (λ=3): c=1, l=3, a=2, b=1; take Mr=0 (ball bearings). Reactions:
RB = [P₂·a + P₁(c+l)]/l = [333.3·2 + 150·4]/3 = 422.2 kgf · RA = [P₂·b − P₁·c]/l = [333.3 − 150]/3 = 61.1 kgf
4 · Support displacements: δB = 422.2×0.1 = 42.2 µm, δA = 61.1×0.15 = 9.17 µm.
5 · Nose deflection (Eq 5.21):
y₂ = δB(1+c/l) + δA(c/l) = 42.2(1.333) + 9.17(0.333) = 59.3 µm
Answer: λopt ≈ 3; nose deflection y₂ ≈ 59 µm (compliance ≈ 0.40 µm/kgf w.r.t. P₁).
Exam tip: front bearing dominates via (1+c/l). Quote λopt from Fig 5.9; bending term y₁ needs spindle EI (dia not given) → omit.

D · Viva Set — Speed, Feed & Time

The viva numericals the prof asks are the simple cutting-speed / feed / machining-time type. Know these cold.

V1 — Cutting speed[2]

Turning Ø50 mm at N=400 rpm. Find cutting speed V.

V = πDN/1000 = π·50·400/1000 = 62.8 m/min
V2 — Spindle speed for a target V[2]

Required cutting speed 25 m/min on Ø80 mm. Find the spindle rpm.

N = 1000V/(πD) = 1000·25/(π·80) = 99.5 ≈ 100 rpm
V3 — Machining time[2]

Turn length L=200 mm, feed f=0.25 mm/rev, N=300 rpm (single pass). Find time.

t = L/(f·N) = 200/(0.25·300) = 2.67 min
V4 — Material removal rate[2]

Turning at V=40 m/min, depth d=2 mm, feed f=0.2 mm/rev. Find MRR.

MRR = V·d·f = 40000·2·0.2 = 16 000 mm³/min
(V in mm/min = 40 m/min × 1000.)
V5 — Geometric-progression speeds[2]

Gearbox: nmin=45 rpm, step ratio φ=1.41, z=6 speeds. List the speeds and find nmax.

Each speed = previous × φ:
45 → 63 → 90 → 125 → 180 → 254 rpm
nmax = 45·1.41⁵ ≈ 252 rpm; range ratio Rn = φ⁵ ≈ 5.6.
