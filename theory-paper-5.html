Drives, Kinematics & Motions

Q1.[4]

Write short notes on individual drive versus group drive in machine tools.

Model answer
Individual drive — one motor drives one machine; independent speed control, higher efficiency, compact, standard on modern machines/CNC. Group (line-shaft) drive — one motor drives several machines through a common line shaft and belts; cheaper historically but obsolete: poor efficiency, no independent control, stops all machines if the shaft fails, cluttered, unsafe. Also note multiple-speed motors, which themselves give a few discrete speeds.
Q2.[4]

Describe the main power-transmission elements used between motor and spindle (mechanical and hydraulic).

Model answer
Mechanical: gears (spur/helical/bevel — speed change, torque), belts (flat/V/timing — smooth, some isolation), friction/jaw clutches (engage-disengage, reversing), power/lead screws (rotary→linear feed), shafts & couplings. Hydraulic: pump (fixed/variable displacement), hydraulic motor or cylinder (rotary/linear output), directional & flow-control valves, accumulator. Hydraulics give smooth stepless motion, overload protection and good damping; used in grinders, broaching, copying.
Q3.[2]

Explain the depth-of-cut motion and where it sits among the machine-tool motions.

Model answer
It is the setting motion that fixes how far the tool penetrates the work (the uncut layer thickness), made before/along the cut. It is an auxiliary/adjusting motion (not a continuous working motion like primary or feed), but it directly sets the cutting load and MRR.
CLO2

Regulation of Speed & Feed

Q4.[5]

Write short notes on the four laws of stepped speed regulation (arithmetic, geometric, harmonic, logarithmic). Which is used and why?

Model answer
  • Arithmetic: constant difference (nₖ₊₁−nₖ=a). Steps too coarse at low rpm, redundant at high rpm — poor.
  • Geometric: constant ratio (nₖ₊₁/nₖ=φ). Gives constant % loss of cutting speed/productivity across the whole range — the standard.
  • Harmonic: constant difference of the served diameter (1/nₖ−1/nₖ₊₁=const). Good at large diameters only; impractical spread.
  • Logarithmic: a special case; not used in practice.
Geometric progression is universally adopted because of the constant relative speed loss and its fit with preferred numbers.
Q5.[3]

Why is speed and feed regulation necessary? State the consequences of having only a single fixed speed.

Model answer
Different materials, diameters and operations (rough vs finish) need different cutting speeds/feeds to protect tool life, surface finish and productivity; a regulated drive lets the operator select the right value. A single fixed speed → wrong cutting speed for most jobs → tool breakage/rapid wear, overheating, poor surface finish and lost productivity.
Q6.[3]

Write a short note on the choice of φ for different sizes/types of machine tool.

Model answer
Small φ (1.06–1.12) → many fine steps, more gears, costlier/complex → used on large, heavy machines (heavy automats φ≈1.12). Large φ (1.58–2.0) → few coarse steps, larger productivity loss → tolerable on small machines. General-purpose machines use the middle values: φ = 1.26, 1.41, 1.58 (most common). Rule: large machine → small φ; small machine → large φ.
CLO3

Guideways

Q7.[3]

Write short notes on the protective and adjusting devices used with slideways.

Model answer
Gibs — tapered/adjustable strips that take up wear clearance and set the fit of the slide. Wipers — felt/rubber scrapers that keep chips and grit off the ways. Way covers — telescopic steel covers, bellows or roller blinds that shield the ways from swarf and coolant. Together they preserve accuracy and extend way life.
Q8.[2]

Note on the materials used for guideways and the reason.

Model answer
Cast iron (integral with the bed) — good damping, cheap, self-lubricating with graphite; hardened-steel inlays or strips for higher wear resistance; non-metallic facings (PTFE/Turcite) to kill stick-slip. Chosen for wear resistance, damping and retention of accuracy.
CLO4

Structures & Spindles

Q9.[4]

State the functions and requirements of a machine-tool structure (bed, column, housing).

Model answer
Functions: support and locate all units, carry static + cutting loads, maintain the geometric relationship between tool and work, provide guideway surfaces and absorb vibration. Requirements: high static & dynamic stiffness, dimensional stability (low residual stress/thermal drift), good damping, wear-resistant way surfaces, and ease of manufacture (casting/welding). Stiffness, not strength, governs.
Q10.[3]

Short note on the tapered joint at the spindle nose and its effect on stiffness.

Model answer
The spindle nose has a standardised taper (e.g. 7:24) seating the tool/centre, so y₃ (taper-joint compliance) adds to the nose deflection. Its stiffness falls as the d/D ratio rises and is very sensitive to taper-angle error — a 30–40′ mismatch can cut stiffness 10–15×. So the taper must be accurate (difference <1′, shank slightly larger angle) for a stiff, repeatable seating.
CLO5

Vibration

Q11.[4]

Write short notes on the effects of vibration on machining.

Model answer
Surface finish — waviness/chatter marks. Dimensional accuracy — taper, ovality, out-of-roundness. Tool life — fluctuating force → flank/crater wear, edge chipping, breakage. Machine — bearing/spindle/guideway damage, loosened fasteners. Also noise, extra heat, and lost productivity (operator must cut conservatively) → higher cost.
Q12.[3]

Short note on vibration monitoring — what sensors are used and what they detect.

Model answer
Sensors: accelerometers, displacement probes, acoustic-emission sensors, force sensors (dynamometers), spindle-current monitors. They detect chatter, bearing defects, tool wear, imbalance and misalignment from the vibration spectrum, enabling predictive maintenance before serious damage.
Q13.[4]

Write short notes on reducing vibration at source.

Model answer
The most effective first step (treats cause, not symptom): balance rotating parts, align shafts/couplings, replace damaged bearings and set correct preload, improve gear quality and reduce backlash, improve lubrication, tighten loose parts, and improve foundation support / fit anti-vibration mounts to block external transmission.
