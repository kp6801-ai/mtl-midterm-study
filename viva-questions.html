1 · Quick Speed / Feed Sums

Write the cutting-speed formula and the rpm form.
Answer
V = πDN/1000 (m/min, D in mm). Rearranged: N = 1000V/(πD).
Ø60 mm bar at 250 rpm — cutting speed?
Answer
V = π·60·250/1000 = 47.1 m/min.
Machining-time formula for turning? Drilling?
Answer
t = L/(f·N), where f = feed mm/rev, L = length (+ approach). Drilling: same, L includes drill-point allowance ≈ 0.3D.
Why geometric progression for spindle speeds, not arithmetic?
Answer
GP keeps the % speed loss between adjacent steps constant across the whole range → equally economical cutting speed at every diameter. φ = (nmax/nmin)^(1/(z−1)); standard φ = 1.06…2.0.
Difference between feed and depth of cut?
Answer
Feed = tool advance per revolution (mm/rev) — sets surface finish/chip width. Depth of cut = radial penetration (mm) — sets MRR and force. MRR = V·f·d.

2 · "Which Topic Does This Come Under?"

A lead-screw problem (average pressure, shearing stress, pitch error) — slideways or power screws?
Answer
Power Screws. The carriage weight + guideway friction only feed the axial load on the screw; the design target is the Acme thread. (Mehta 4.9.)
A ball-recirculating screw-nut (ball load, L₁₀ life) — slideways or power screws?
Answer
Power Screws (ball-screw). Don't confuse with "ball-recirculating guideways" = rolling linear guides, which are slideways. (Mehta 4.10.)
Saddle lift-off (d/b ratio) on V+flat ways — which topic?
Answer
Design of Slideways — slideway force equilibrium / tipping geometry. (Mehta 4.1.)
Machine-tool compliance / spindle-nose deflection — which topic?
Answer
Calculation of deflection & associated problems (Ch5). Covers 5.1, 5.2, 5.3. CLO4 — stiffness criterion.
Hollow base "maximum angle of slope" / table "reduced stiffness" — which topic?
Answer
Design of Bases & Tables (Mehta §3.9). Questions 3.8, 3.9. Plate-on-elastic-foundation / stiffener analysis.
What is the design criterion for machine-tool structures and spindles — strength or stiffness?
Answer
Stiffness. Accuracy fails on deflection long before stress; all steels share nearly the same E, so a stronger alloy gives no rigidity gain.

3 · Guideway Concepts

Three functions of a guideway?
Answer
Accurate guided motion · carry cutting/weight loads · maintain tool–workpiece alignment.
What is stick-slip and how do you cure it?
Answer
Jerky low-speed motion because μstatic > μkinetic. Cure: anti-friction (PTFE/Turcite) facings, rolling or hydrostatic ways, anti-stick-slip oil, stiffer drive.
Flat vs V vs dovetail — one line each.
Answer
Flat — high load, needs gib. V — self-aligning, wear-compensating. Dovetail — compact, resists lift-off/overturning (tool slides).
Which slideway combination gives the least radial deflection — and why?
Answer
V–V: symmetric load split, no overturning couple → least radial deflection. (Mehta 4.3, proof by comparing reaction-induced deflections.)

4 · Structures, Spindle & Vibration

Why a closed box for a bed but an I-section for an arm?
Answer
Closed box = huge enclosed area → high torsional rigidity (Bredt). I-section = material in flanges far from neutral axis → max bending I per weight. (Mehta 3.3.)
What does bearing preload do to spindle stiffness?
Answer
Removes clearance (no jump on load reversal) and shifts support from simply-supported toward clamped → stiffer, lower nose deflection. Excess preload → heat + short bearing life, so keep it optimum.
Name the three classes of machine-tool vibration.
Answer
Free/transient · forced (unbalance, gears, interrupted cut) · self-excited chatter (regenerative).
How do you suppress chatter?
Answer
Raise stiffness & damping, add a tuned-mass/dynamic absorber, isolate the foundation, change speed to a stable lobe, use variable-pitch/helix tools.
